Energy & Fuel

EU Oil Bulletin · Week of May 2026
⚡ Italy has among the highest fuel taxes in Europe (accise) — more than €0.73/L on petrol. Electricity prices are also above EU average (~0.29 €/kWh household) due to high gas dependency. Renewables (solar + hydro) now provide ~42% of electricity generation in 2025, but Italy still imports ~75% of its energy.

20 regions · ISTAT 2024–2025
🗺 Italy has 20 regions, 5 of which are autonomous (Sicilia, Sardegna, Trentino-Alto Adige, Valle d'Aosta, Friuli-Venezia Giulia). The North–South economic divide is one of the deepest in the EU: per-capita GDP in Trentino is ~€48k, while in Calabria it's barely ~€20k. Unemployment in the South (Sud) is 3–4× higher than in the North.

Housing — Buy & Rent

OMI / Agenzia delle Entrate / Idealista · Q4 2025
ℹ️ Below: 🛒 Buy cards show sale price per m² (OMI — Osservatorio del Mercato Immobiliare, transaction-based). 🔑 Rent cards show total monthly rent for a ~T2 apartment (~70 m²). Construction cost is not shown here (~1,500 €/m² in IT). The North–South housing divide is striking: Milan is 5× more expensive than Palermo.
🏠 The Italian housing paradox: Despite high prices in Milan and Rome, the national average sale price is the lowest among large EU economies (~2,100 €/m²). The South has plenty of empty stock (depopulation, "1€ houses"), while the North faces severe shortages. Home-ownership rate: ~73% — among the highest in the EU.

Income & Work

ISTAT / Eurostat · 2025–2026
⚠️ Italy has NO statutory minimum wage. Wages are set via CCNL (Contratto Collettivo Nazionale di Lavoro) — sector-specific collective agreements covering ~80% of workers. De-facto minimum varies by sector: ~€6/hour for food service, ~€9/hour for metalworking. A proposal for a €9/hour statutory minimum was rejected by Parliament in 2023.

Historical Trends

10-year view · 2016–2026
Benzina rose from 1.52 €/L in 2016 to 1.94 €/L in 2026 — a +28% increase. Italy has among the highest fuel taxes in Europe: accise + IVA account for ~57% of the price at the pump. The 2022 energy crisis prompted a temporary excise cut (−€0.30/L) which was phased out in 2023.
Fuel price evolution (€/L)
Milano peaked at 5,700 €/m² in 2024 and is now slightly higher in 2025 — 2.7× the national median. Italian housing has been remarkably stable nationally: prices rose only +10% over the 2016–2025 decade vs +50% in Germany or +80% in Portugal. The cause: chronic stagnation in the South offsetting price growth in Milan/Rome.
Milano vs national median (€/m²)
Italy is the only major EU economy where real wages are LOWER than in 2008. ISTAT data shows nominal gross wages rose from ~2,300 €/mo (2016) to ~2,850 €/mo (2025) — but adjusted for inflation, purchasing power is essentially flat. The OECD reports Italy as the only EU country with declining real wages in the long run.
Salary vs housing price
Real wages (2008 = 100)
Unemployment fell sharply from 11.7% in 2016 to a multi-decade low of 5.2% by Mar 2026 — a remarkable improvement. The fall is partly real (Meloni govt + Quota 100 retirements) and partly demographic (shrinking labour force). The North/South divide remains: Trentino ~3% vs Calabria ~17%.
Unemployment rate (%)
Italy's public debt rose from 132% of GDP in 2016 to ~135% in 2025 — temporarily spiking to 155% during Covid. The 2nd highest debt-to-GDP in the EU after Greece. Bond spread vs Bunds (10y BTP-Bund) has stayed around 100bp through 2024-2025, well below the 2011 crisis levels.

Climate

ISPRA / Servizio Meteorologico · 2024–2025
🌡 Italy spans Alpine to Mediterranean climates. The peninsula has warmed +2.4°C since 1900 — faster than the global average. 2025 was again among the warmest years on record. Southern Italy faces increasing droughts (Sicilia, Puglia); the Alps have lost ~50% of glacial ice volume since 1950.

Healthcare

ISTAT / Ministero della Salute · 2024–2025
🏥 Italy has the Servizio Sanitario Nazionale (SSN) — universal, mostly tax-funded healthcare. Italy spends 9.0% of GDP on healthcare — below the EU big-economy avg. Life expectancy (83.5 yrs) is the highest in the EU, but the system suffers from regional disparities (Northern regions outperform Southern ones) and chronic underfunding.

34th most peaceful globally · 16th in the EU · IEP GPI 2024 · pulled down by organised crime presence
🛡 Italy is generally safe for everyday life. Petty crime (pickpocketing, scams) is significant in tourist hubs (Roma, Milano, Napoli). Organised crime (Mafia, Camorra, 'Ndrangheta, Sacra Corona Unita) remains a structural issue, especially in the South — though direct violence has fallen sharply since the 1990s peak.
