Historical Trends

10-year view · 2016–2026
SP 95 rose from 1.35 €/L in 2016 to 1.74 €/L in 2026 — a +29% increase. France's fuel prices stayed consistently below most EU neighbours thanks to lower distribution margins, though still ~20% above 2019 pre-crisis levels.
Fuel price evolution (€/L)
Paris peaked at 11,200 €/m² in 2022 and has corrected −12% to 9,800 €/m² in 2025 — still 3× the national median. Nationally, prices rose from 2,400 €/m² in 2016, a +32% increase. Higher interest rates since 2022 triggered the first broad correction in 15 years.
Paris vs national median (€/m²)
The SMIC minimum wage rose from 1,466 €/month in 2016 to 1,802 €/month in 2026 — a +23% increase. Several automatic "coups de pouce" during high-inflation periods (2022–2023) boosted the SMIC faster than inflation. Average salaries grew from ~30,000 €/yr to ~40,800 €/yr.
Salary vs housing price
SMIC evolution (€/mo)
Unemployment fell from 10.0% in 2016 to a low of 7.1% in Q4 2024, then rose to 7.9% in Q1 2026 amid slowing growth. The Covid peak of 9.2% in 2020 recovered quickly. Youth unemployment remains elevated at 21.1%, well above the national rate.
Unemployment rate (%)
France's public debt rose from 97% of GDP in 2016 to 112% in 2025, with Covid adding ~15pp. Unlike Spain, France has not reduced debt significantly post-pandemic, triggering EU monitoring. The budget deficit has been above 3% every year since 2019.
