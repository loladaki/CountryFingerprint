attention

Warnings, achievements and trends worth watching closely — across all countries we track.

27th
Housing affordability · EU ranking

Portugal ranks last in the EU for housing affordability. Median home costs over 10× the average annual salary — the worst ratio in the bloc.

🇵🇹 Portugal · OECD 2025
🧑
33.6yrs
Avg. age leaving parental home

7 years later than the EU average of 26.3 years. Low wages, scarce rentals and high prices trap young Portuguese at home far longer than their European peers.

🇵🇹 Portugal · Eurostat 2023
⛽
1.958€/L
Diesel price · Apr 2026

Near historic highs due to the Iran conflict. In March 2026, diesel hit 2.037 €/L — the most expensive ever recorded in Portugal. Gasoline is just behind at 2.009 €/L.

🇵🇹 Portugal · EU Oil Bulletin
🌍
7th
Global Peace Index · World ranking

3rd most peaceful in the EU. Over 81% of Portuguese feel safe walking alone at night — 2nd highest in Europe. A remarkable achievement for any country.

🇵🇹 Portugal · IEP GPI 2024
👶
18.1%
Youth unemployment (15–24)

3× the general unemployment rate and above the EU average. Despite falling from 28% in 2016, youth unemployment remains one of Portugal's most stubborn problems.

🇵🇹 Portugal · INE Mar 2026
🌱
67%
Renewable electricity share

4th in the EU for renewable electricity (EU avg: 44%). Portugal targets 80% by 2026 — powered by wind, solar and hydro. One of Europe's greenest energy grids.

Methodology

Data you
can trust

Every number on EU Stats comes from an official, verifiable source. We never estimate or extrapolate without disclosure.

  • 📡
    Live where possibleFuel prices update weekly via the EU Oil Bulletin. Brent updates every 30 min via Yahoo Finance.
  • 📅
    Always datedEvery data point shows its source and publication date. You always know how fresh the data is.
  • 🔗
    Linked to sourceEvery metric links to the original dataset so you can verify it yourself.
  • 🇪🇺
    Official sources onlyEurostat, national statistics institutes, ECB, OECD, IEP and EU agencies.
