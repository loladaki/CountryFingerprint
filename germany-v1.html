16 federal states · Destatis 2024–2025

East–west gap is striking: Leipzig at 2,600 €/m² vs Frankfurt at 6,100 €/m². Rent caps (Mietpreisbremse) exist in many cities but loopholes are common.

Historical Trends

10-year view · 2016–2026
Super E5 rose from 1.34 €/L in 2016 to 1.79 €/L in 2026 — a +34% increase. The brief 2022 "Tankrabatt" subsidy capped prices, but post-crisis Germany now sits near the EU median. Fuel taxes (Energiesteuer + VAT) account for ~55% of the price at the pump.
Fuel price evolution (€/L)
Munich peaked at 11,000 €/m² in 2022 and corrected −7% to 10,200 €/m² in 2025 — still 2.8× the national median. National prices rose from 2,400 €/m² in 2016, a +50% increase. The 2022–2024 ECB rate-hiking cycle triggered the first broad housing correction since reunification (−5% nationally in 2023).
Munich vs national median (€/m²)
The federal minimum wage (Mindestlohn) was introduced only in 2015 at 8.50 €/hr. After the major bump to 12.00 €/hr in October 2022 (statutory raise), it reached 12.82 €/hr in 2026. Median household gross income rose from ~3,300 €/mo (2016) to ~4,400 €/mo (2026), tracking inflation more closely than wages did in 2022–2023.
Salary vs housing price
Mindestlohn evolution (€/hr)
Unemployment fell from 6.4% in 2016 to a 30-year low of 3.0% in 2022, then rose to 4.0% by Feb 2026 amid the energy crisis aftermath and weak industrial output. Germany weathered Covid with very limited unemployment thanks to Kurzarbeit (short-time work). Youth unemployment at 6.5% remains the lowest in the EU thanks to the dual apprenticeship system.
Unemployment rate (%)
Germany's public debt rose from 69% of GDP in 2016 to 63% in 2025 (briefly to 70% during Covid). The constitutional Schuldenbremse (debt brake) caps structural deficits at 0.35% of GDP — the strictest fiscal rule in the EU. This sound balance sheet contrasts sharply with France or Italy but limits stimulus capacity during downturns.

Climate

DWD · 2024–2025
🌡 Germany has a predominantly temperate oceanic climate in the west, transitioning to continental in the east. Average temperatures have risen +1.9°C since the late 19th century — among the fastest in Europe. 2025 was the joint-warmest year on record alongside 2022 and 2018.

Healthcare

Destatis / GKV / Eurostat · 2024–2025
🏥 Germany has a dual public–private health insurance system (gesetzliche + private Krankenversicherung). It spends 12.7% of GDP on healthcare — the highest in the EU. The system delivers strong access (5 days median wait for specialists) but is under pressure from an ageing population and rising contribution rates.
